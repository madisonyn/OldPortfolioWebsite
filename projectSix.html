Richmond Greenway | Wayfinding, Signage, and Visual Identity

Problem

After visiting the Richmond Greenway, it was insightful to observe the successful developments and areas of improvement that could be transformed to better serve the community and enhance involvement. 42nd Street Pocket Park is a sector along the greenway that appears to be neglected, yet it has the potential to be a space that offers safety and ownership to Richmond residents and frequent visitors. The space is cozy and provides an opportunity for play and community events, however, it currently lacks active use which can be attributed to a lack of upkeep, knowledge of the existing space, and a cohesive and distinct visual identity.

Objective

My project aims to develop appropriate and attractive signage and wayfinding material with a streamlined visual identity for 42nd Street Pocket Park to increase activation of the space and produce more user-friendly areas along the greenway.
My project consists of a few different pieces that rely on each other to develop an impactful visual identity and an activated space for 42nd Street Pocket Park. The main park sign is a larger statement piece that marks the arrival of the park while acting as its main signifier. With a large, visible sign, people will be more aware of the intentional use of the space. The visual graphic identity is strongly emphasized on this sign. The visual itself is colorful and playful to cultivate a friendly atmosphere around and within the park.
My second piece of signage addresses navigational confusion and to increase awareness of the park, I created a series of wayfinding signage that directs people on the greenway to the park. The signage is intended to appeal to all users on the greenway, either on foot, on a bike, or even on a skateboard! Informationally, the sign allows greenway users to easily determine their current distance from the park and the estimated amount of time it would take to arrive there on their specific mode of transportation. It also features both English and Spanish to increase accessibility and inclusivity. The top also has an element of the park’s visual graphic identity that can be found on all signage material for 42nd Street Pocket Park.
The last piece of signage is a continually updating or changing events poster and scheduling calendar to inform visitors of events occurring during the month at the park or in other areas around the greenway. These could be volunteering events with Groundwork Richmond or other affiliated stakeholders of the greenway. This signage serves to engage the community in events that support the growth of their space to create positive returns back to them. As a temporary part of the park, it could be pinned to a wall for ease of changing. It could also have it’s own built structure or mantle that contains the posters which can be easily switched for the following month’s events.
