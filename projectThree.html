CrunchCorn | Logo and Package Design

In this project, I was asked to create an insect-based food company and design a logo, a graphic standard with collateral material for the company. Additionally, I also designed and created a shelf-ready prototype of the package design for my product.

My process started with searching for a company identity, using simple sketches and word association to flesh out ideas. I decided on an insect-popcorn mixture, with popcorn made from dried insect powder and settled on “CrunchCorn” for its catchy alliteration and fun spin-off of “popcorn.” In the burgeoning industry of insect food products, Crunch Corn provides a unique option of crunchy insects, such as mealworms and crickets, in combination with popcorn coated with insect dust. Our initiative at Crunch Corn is to understand consumer concerns regarding insect-based foods and we strive to offer a product that is easily approachable.

The final logo features a 4 inch and 1 inch version for different sized products or additional marketing. They both incorporate a gradient purple effect and rounded lettering to create a playful and engaging effect. The graphic standard serves to set the tone for the brand identity and establish a guideline for typography, color palette, logo specifications, and the business system.

In my package design, I aimed to design a container that would be protective, secure, and also allow for resealability to maintain freshness. I decided on a hexagonal shaped container with long protruding edges that act as a mechanism of protection to the contents inside if the package were dropped or crushed. It also features a removable lid and two inside flaps that provide additional protection from outside elements.
